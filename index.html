Strategy Guide
Your personalised NEET PG 2026 battle plan
🚨 Pharmacology — rescue plan
  • Do Pharma in Block 3 EVERY single day
  • 200 Pharma MCQs daily throughout Rev 1
  • Focus: mechanism + prototype drug + adverse effects
  • High-yield: ANS, CVS, CNS, Antimicrobials, Cancer drugs
  • Rev 2: only one-liners and drug tables
👁 Ophthalmology — fast track
  • Split into 4 sessions across Rev 1
  • Priority: IOP values, visual acuity, field defects
  • Glaucoma — open vs closed angle, drugs
  • Retina — CRVO, CRAO, DR grading, AMD
  • 150 MCQs per Opthal session minimum
📖 The 60-30-10 rule
  • 60% → strong subjects (stay sharp, high marks)
  • 30% → Pharmacology + Ophthalmology (boost zone)
  • 10% → Random mixed MCQs
  • Never let Pharma + Opthal drop below 150 MCQs/day
🎯 Mock test protocol
  • Every mock = strict exam conditions, no phone
  • After mock: 3-hour mandatory analysis
  • Log every wrong: knowledge gap / silly / guess
  • Track error rate by subject across all mocks
  • Target: M1→370, M4→500, M6→560, M8→640+
📋 3-slot daily structure
  • Slot 1 — Primary subject of the day (Prepladder notes)
  • Slot 2 — Supporting subject or continuation + 200 MCQs
  • Slot 3 — DAILY weak drill: Ophthalmology OR Pharmacology (alternating)
  • Slot 3 never changes — weak subjects get drilled every single day
  • Subject order: Patho → Micro → Medicine → Surgery → OBG → Paed → PSM → Anat → Physio → Biochem → ENT → Opthal → FMT → Psych → Pharma → Radio → Ortho → Anaes → Derma
⚡ Revision 2 rules
  • NO full note re-reading — only one-liners, tables
  • Each subject max 1 day in Rev 2
  • MCQ volume → 900 questions/day
  • Active recall only — close notes, test yourself first
  • No new topic after 25 days before exam
💤 Non-negotiables
  • 6.5–7 hrs sleep minimum — memory consolidates here
  • 30 min break after every 3-hr block
  • No screen after 11:30 PM
  • Eat every 4 hrs — brain uses 25% of glucose
  • Final 5 days: reduce intensity, 7+ hrs sleep
